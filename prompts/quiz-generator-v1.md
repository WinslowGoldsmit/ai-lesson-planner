# Topic Lesson Plan: T1 — Introduction to Motion in a Plane and Vectors

## TOPIC INFORMATION

**Topic ID:** T1

**Topic Name:** Introduction to Motion in a Plane and Vectors

**Duration:** 1 Period (30 Minutes)

---

## ==================================================

## GENERAL OBJECTIVE

Students will understand the fundamental difference between one-dimensional and two-dimensional motion, recognize vectors as quantities with both magnitude and direction, and represent vectors in a plane using appropriate notation and diagrams.

---

## ==================================================

## SPECIFIC OBJECTIVES
*(Revised Bloom's Taxonomy)*

### Remember
* Students will recall the definitions of scalar and vector quantities and list at least three examples of each.

### Understand
* Students will explain why motion in a plane requires two coordinates (x and y) to describe position, using a real-life example.

### Apply
* Students will draw a vector to represent a given displacement or velocity in a plane, showing correct magnitude and direction.

### Analyze
* Students will compare one-dimensional motion with two-dimensional motion and identify the additional information needed to describe motion in a plane.

### Evaluate
* Students will assess whether a given physical quantity is a scalar or a vector and justify their classification with reasoning.

### Create
* Students will design a simple diagram showing the path of an object moving in a plane and label the position vectors at two different points.

---

## ==================================================

## PRIOR KNOWLEDGE

* Difference between distance and displacement
* Difference between speed and velocity
* Basic concept of scalar and vector quantities
* Familiarity with coordinate axes (x-axis and y-axis)
* Understanding of one-dimensional motion along a straight line
* Basic geometry (points, lines, angles)

---

## ==================================================

## 5E LESSON PLAN

### Engage (Time: 4 minutes)

**"The Walk to School" Story**

Ask students: *"Imagine you walk 3 meters east from your desk to the door, then 4 meters north to the window. Are you now 7 meters away from your desk?"*

Allow brief responses. Most will say "no." Ask: *"Then how far are you?"*

Introduce the idea: You moved in **two directions**, not just one. This is motion in a plane — and we need a new way to describe it.

---

### Explore (Time: 6 minutes)

**"Vector Walk" Activity**

* Give each student a small grid paper (5×5 squares).
* Mark a starting point as "Home."
* Give instructions: "Move 3 squares right, then 2 squares up. Mark your new position."
* Ask: "How many squares did you actually travel from Home in a straight line?"
* Let students count or estimate. Guide them to see that the straight-line path is different from the sum of individual steps.
* Introduce the term: the straight-line path is the **resultant displacement vector**.

---

### Explain (Time: 10 minutes)

**Core Concepts:**

1. **Motion in a Plane vs. Straight Line**
   - One-dimensional motion: only forward or backward (like walking along a straight corridor).
   - Two-dimensional motion: can move forward, backward, left, right (like walking on a playground).

2. **Vectors in a Plane**
   - A vector has **magnitude** (how much) and **direction** (which way).
   - Represented by an arrow: length = magnitude, arrowhead = direction.
   - Examples: displacement, velocity, acceleration, force.

3. **Position Vector**
   - If a point P has coordinates (x, y), its position vector is the arrow from the origin to P.
   - Example: A student at (3, 4) on a grid has a position vector of magnitude 5 units (using Pythagoras).

4. **Scalar vs. Vector Review**
   - Scalar: mass, temperature, distance, speed (only magnitude).
   - Vector: displacement, velocity, acceleration, force (magnitude + direction).

**Quick Example:** A bird flies 10 m/s at 30° above horizontal. Draw the arrow. Ask: "Is this one number or two pieces of information?" (Answer: two — speed and angle.)

---

### Elaborate (Time: 6 minutes)

**"Vector or Not?" Quick-Fire Game**

Call out quantities one by one. Students raise:
- **Right hand** if vector
- **Left hand** if scalar

Examples:
- 50 km/h north → Vector
- 25°C → Scalar
- 5 kg → Scalar
- 9.8 m/s² downward → Vector
- 100 meters walked → Scalar
- 100 meters northeast → Vector

**Discussion:** Ask one student to explain their choice for "100 meters northeast" vs. "100 meters walked." Reinforce that direction makes the difference.

---

### Evaluate (Time: 4 minutes)

**Exit Ticket — Answer on a slip of paper:**

1. Define a vector in your own words.
2. A car moves from point A (2, 3) to point B (5, 7) on a coordinate grid. Is this motion in one dimension or two? Why?
3. Classify: "The wind is blowing at 20 km/h from the north." Is this a scalar or vector? Justify.

Collect slips as students leave. Review to check understanding of vector definition, planar motion recognition, and scalar-vector distinction.

---

## ==================================================

## HOME ASSIGNMENT

1. **Observation Task:** List five examples of motion in a plane you observe at home or in your neighborhood (e.g., a fan blade, a thrown ball, an ant on a wall). For each, write one sentence describing the two directions involved.

2. **Drawing Task:** On graph paper, draw a coordinate system. Mark your home at the origin (0, 0). Mark your school at (4, 3). Draw the position vector from home to school and estimate its length using the Pythagorean theorem.

3. **Conceptual Question:** Explain why "10 meters" is a scalar but "10 meters east" is a vector. Use the words *magnitude* and *direction* in your answer.

---

*This lesson plan is designed for a single 30-minute period, focusing on building foundational understanding of vectors and planar motion through active participation and real-life connections.*
